The Perfect Pour-Over Technique

A meditative guide to mastering the nuances of bloom, agitation, and the rhythmic flow of a morning ritual.

Elias Thorne

Lead Roaster & Sensory Analyst

October 24, 2023

The Setup

  • V60 Ceramic Dripper
  • Estate Filter Paper
  • Gooseneck Kettle
  • 20g Single Origin Beans
  • 300ml Filtered Water

Time & Temp

Brew Time 3:15 min
Water Temp 94°C / 201°F

The pour-over is more than a delivery system for caffeine; it is a dialogue between the roaster's intent and the brewer's patience. In this guide, we strip away the technical jargon to focus on the sensory cues that define a world-class cup.

The Preparation: Precision in Grind

Success begins before the first drop of water touches the grounds. For a V60 or Chemex, aim for a grind consistency resembling kosher salt. Too fine, and you'll find a bitter, over-extracted finish; too coarse, and the water will race through, leaving a sour, thin profile behind.

The secret is not in the pour, but in the pause. The bloom is where the coffee reveals its soul.

Step 1: The Bloom

Gently pour 40g of water over the grounds, ensuring all are saturated. Watch as the coffee expands—this is the release of carbon dioxide. We call this the bloom. Wait exactly 30 seconds. This phase ensures the water can penetrate the cells of the bean uniformly in the next steps.

Active degassing during the bloom phase.

The ideal medium-fine grind size.

Step 2: The Main Extraction

Using a slow, circular motion, begin pouring the remaining water. Start from the center and spiral outwards, avoiding the very edges of the filter. The goal is a flat coffee bed once the water has passed through. If you see high "walls" of coffee on the sides, your technique may be too aggressive.

Maintain a steady, thin stream. The turbulence caused by your pour is what agitates the grounds, encouraging an even extraction of those delicate floral and citrus notes Estate & Bean is known for.
