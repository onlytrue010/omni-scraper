The Gedeo Zone: Ethiopia

High-altitude mist, volcanic soil, and the ancient birthplace of the Arabica bean. Journey into the heart of Yirgacheffe.

The Terroir

Mist-Clad Slopes & Iron-Rich Earth

In the southern highlands of Ethiopia lies the Gedeo Zone, a region synonymous with some of the world's most aromatic coffees. Here, the altitude reaches a staggering 2,200 meters above sea level, where the air is cool and the ripening process of the coffee cherry is slow and deliberate.

The soil is deeply weathered, reddish-brown, and rich in minerals—a result of ancient volcanic activity. This unique geology, combined with the dense forest canopy, creates a micro-climate where heirloom varieties thrive without the need for artificial intervention.

Ancestral Heirloom

Unlike many other regions, Gedeo relies almost exclusively on indigenous wild varieties, often referred to simply as "Ethiopian Heirlooms."

The Farmers

Stewards of the Canopy

In Gedeo, coffee isn't grown in massive plantations. It's grown in "Garden Coffee" systems—small plots surrounding the family home. The farmers here are masters of agroforestry, cultivating coffee alongside food crops under the shade of native trees.

We partner with local cooperatives that ensure fair wages and reinvestment into the Gedeo community. Every cherry is hand-picked at its absolute peak of ripeness, a labor-intensive process that preserves the integrity of the bean.

Locating the Source

The Gedeo Zone is a administrative zone in the Southern Nations, Nationalities, and Peoples' Region of Ethiopia. Narrow and mountainous, it is named for the Gedeo people whose homelands lie in this zone.
